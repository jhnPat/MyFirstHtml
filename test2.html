EatBulagaOP-1982-1988



On May 18, 1982, as part of the television show Eat... Bulaga! The theme song was initially played in full at the DOMSAT Launch event at The Plaza in Makati, Metro Manila, which also led to the launching of the newest member of the EB family â€“ Coney Reyes-Mumar.






EatBulagaOP-1988-1995



Little Miss Philippines runner-up Aiza Seguerra joined the main cast in 1987 and in 1988, the lyrics mentioning the names of the primary hosts were updated to include Aiza; and the third line of the third stanza was also revised, replacing "Apat silang nagbibigay" with "Silang lahat ay nagbibigay."






EatBulagaOP-1995-1998



As new hosts were added to the show, such as Jimmy Santos, Lani Mercado, Ces Quesada, Plinky Recto, Nova Villa, Ruby Rodriguez, Christine Jacob, Rio Diaz, and Lady Lee, it became clear that the theme was becoming out of date, especially with the lines mentioning the hosts- Coney had left Eat... Bulaga! in 1991. Only in 1995, on the same day when EB departed ABS-CBN for GMA, were the lyrics of the theme song's third stanza updated to reflect that increased number, changing "Si Aiza at si Coney" to "Barkada'y dumarami."


EatBulagaOP-1998-2023



The last major update to the lyrics was made in 1998, to commemorate the centennial of Philippine independence. It is reported to have been prompted by a letter from the government of Batanes (the country's northernmost province) expressing dissatisfaction with the theme tune, which had been broadcast there as well. As a result, the first phrase, "Mula Aparri hanggang Jolo" (which is now an idiom referring to the entire Philippines), was modified to "Mula Batanes hanggang Jolo" in response, and now keeping with the country's "wholeness."

EatBulagaOP-2023-Present



TVJ hosted "Eat Bulaga!" for 43 years before leaving the show in May 2023 due to a disagreement with TAPE Inc. They established their own production company, TVJ Productions Inc., and launched a successor program on TV5 called "E.A.T." The new theme song for "E.A.T." includes changes, with "Eat Bulaga" being replaced by "E.A.T." TVJ aimed to regain the original title, which TAPE Inc. still uses for its noontime show on GMA Network, asserting that the TV5 program is the true "Eat Bulaga." The hosts also do a "peek-a-boo" hand gesture and encourage the audience to sing the phrase "Bulaga."
